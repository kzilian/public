Digital Mannerism
or: a postmodern thriller (2023-2024)
Digital Mannerism is a body of work I developed on the relation of reference-based design methods and the topic of the original.
The project explores the interplay of style and order and brings us closer to the wonderful world of mannerism.
The attempts of ordering styles and breaking diffusion models produced in addition to the 3D printed fragments - a scarf.
Beyond composing these essays in a magazine, the project introduces the contemporary "diffusion" workflow.
Architectural elements and other explorations based with fine-tuned text-to-image referencing (textual inversion and stable diffusion).
These "diffusion based" references were then translated with various image-to-3D models such as Zero-1-to-3, Dream-Gaussian
and the depth-map model MiDaS. The 3D models were then rendered out in Blender.

A selection of the architectural element series has been brought to life with full color SLS prints.
Project supervision by the Chair of Digital Architectonics at ETH Zurich.
